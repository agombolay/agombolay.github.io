Hello, my name is Alli Gombolay, and I am a second year Bioinformatics Ph.D. student in the School of Biological Sciences at Georgia Tech. I earned my B.S. in Biology (minor in Spanish) with Highest Honor from Georgia Tech in 2013. Afterward, I earned my M.P.H. in Epidemiology from Emory University in 2015.

My Ph.D. research focuses on mapping the incorporation of ribonucleotides (the subunits of RNA) that can become mistakenly embedded in DNA by DNA polymerases or as a result of oxidative stress.
